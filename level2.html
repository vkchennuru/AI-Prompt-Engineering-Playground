Chain-of-Thought (CoT) Prompting

CoT prompting asks the model to reason step-by-step before giving a final answer. It dramatically improves accuracy on math, logic, and complex reasoning tasks.

‚úó Without CoT
Roger has 5 tennis balls. He buys 2 cans of balls with 3 balls each. How many does he have? Answer: 11
No reasoning shown. Model may miss steps on harder problems.
‚úì With CoT
Roger has 5 tennis balls. He buys 2 cans of balls with 3 balls each. How many does he have? Let's think step by step. Reasoning: Roger starts with 5 balls. He buys 2 cans √ó 3 balls = 6 new balls. Total: 5 + 6 = 11. Answer: 11
Explicit reasoning ensures accuracy. Critical for multi-step problems.

CoT Trigger Phrases

Classic
"Let's think step by step."
Detailed
"Think through this carefully, showing your reasoning."
Structured
"First [X], then [Y], finally give the answer."
Explicit
"Before answering, list your reasoning steps."

Role Prompting

Assigning a persona to the model shifts its vocabulary, knowledge depth, and communication style. It's one of the highest-impact single changes you can make.

‚úó No Role
Explain quantum entanglement.
Generic textbook answer. No calibration to audience or expertise level.
‚úì With Role
You are a physics professor who specializes in making complex concepts accessible. Explain quantum entanglement to a curious 12-year-old using only everyday analogies.
Role + audience = perfectly calibrated response.

Role Formula

You are a [JOB TITLE] with [X years] of experience in [SPECIFIC DOMAIN]. Your communication style is [STYLE]. Your audience is [TARGET AUDIENCE]. [TASK]

Controlling Output Format

Explicitly specifying output format is one of the most underused techniques. The model can produce almost any structured format if you ask correctly.

JSON
"Return as JSON with keys: name, age, skills"
Markdown Table
"Format as a markdown table with columns: Feature | Pro | Con"
Numbered List
"Give me exactly 5 numbered points"
CSV
"Output as CSV: name,price,category"
Structured Text
"Use headings: Summary, Details, Next Steps"
Code Block
"Return only the code, no explanation"
‚úó No Format
List programming languages and their uses
Will produce a wall of text or inconsistent formatting.
‚úì Format Specified
List 5 programming languages and their primary use case. Format as JSON array: [{"language": "...", "use_case": "...", "difficulty": "beginner|intermediate|advanced"}] Return only the JSON, no explanation.
Predictable, parseable, directly usable output.
